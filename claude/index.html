dyeary

Ask Claude about your own journal

The dyeary connector for Claude

dyeary can plug into Claude as a connector. Once connected, Claude can look things up in your journal when you ask — your moods, your people, the things you keep coming back to — and answer with your own words as the source.

What was I anxious about last month, and did it pass?
Summarize how the job search has been going, in my own words.
When did I last feel really good, and what was happening that day?

Install in Claude (web, desktop, or mobile)

  1. Open claude.ai → Settings → Connectors.
  2. Choose Add custom connector.
  3. Name it dyeary and paste this URL: https://dyeary-backend.onrender.com/mcp
  4. Click Add, then Connect. A dyeary sign-in page opens — sign in with the same account you use in the app (Google or email).
  5. That's it. In any chat, Claude can now search your journal when you ask it to.

Custom connectors are available on Claude's paid plans. The connection follows your Claude account, so connecting once covers web, desktop, and mobile.

Install in Claude Code

One command in your terminal:

claude mcp add --transport http dyeary https://dyeary-backend.onrender.com/mcp

Then run /mcp inside Claude Code to sign in.

What Claude can and cannot do

  • Read-only, always. The connector can search entries, read an entry, summarize your moods over time, and list what you've said about a person or topic. It cannot write, edit, or delete anything — the capability simply doesn't exist on the connection.
  • Only your account. Every request is tied to your sign-in. There is no way to reach anyone else's journal.
  • Only when you ask. Claude queries dyeary during your conversation, in response to you. Nothing syncs in the background.

Privacy, plainly

Whatever Claude reads from your journal becomes part of that Claude conversation, under Anthropic's privacy terms — the same as if you had pasted the entry yourself. dyeary's own promises hold on our side: entries stay encrypted at rest, nothing is used to train AI, and the connector adds no tracking. See how privacy works in dyeary.

Disconnecting

Remove the connector any time in Claude's Settings → Connectors. Access expires on its own shortly after; nothing about your journal changes.

If something doesn't work

  • "Sign-in expired" during connect — go back to Claude and hit Connect again; the sign-in page is only valid for a few minutes.
  • Claude says it can't reach dyeary — disconnect and reconnect the connector to refresh its access.
